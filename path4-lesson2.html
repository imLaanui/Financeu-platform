Savings Priorities: Emergency Fund First

Screen 1 of 8

Understanding Savings Priorities

The Problem

People try to save for everything at once and end up saving for nothing

Why It Matters

Following the right order prevents financial disasters and builds lasting security

What You'll Learn

Why emergency funds come BEFORE other savings goals and how to prioritize

Screen 2 of 8

Core Concept: The Savings Priority Pyramid

Not all savings goals are equal. Following the right order protects you from setbacks while building wealth.

1 Emergency Fund ($500-$1,000 starter)

Your first priority. This prevents one unexpected expense from derailing everything.

2 High-Interest Debt Payoff

Credit cards charging 20%+ APR destroy wealth faster than you can build it.

3 Full Emergency Fund (3-6 months expenses)

Complete your safety net before other goals. This is financial foundation.

4 Retirement & Long-term Goals

Only AFTER securing your emergency fund should you focus on retirement, investing, or dream purchases.

Real-World Example: Two Approaches

Meet Taylor and Jordan, both earning $3,500/month with $500 to save:

Taylor's Mistake: Saving for Everything

  • $150 to vacation fund
  • $150 to new car fund
  • $100 to retirement
  • $100 to emergency fund

Result: After 2 months, Taylor's car breaks down ($800 repair). With only $200 in emergency savings, Taylor uses credit cards at 22% APR. The $800 repair costs $1,100 in total after interest. All other savings goals stall.

Jordan's Smart Priority: Emergency Fund First

  • $500 to emergency fund (100% focus)
  • No other savings yet

Result: After 2 months, Jordan has $1,000 emergency fund. When the same $800 car repair happens, Jordan pays cash, avoiding debt completely. Emergency fund saved Jordan $300 in interest AND kept other goals on track.

The Lesson: Focusing on one priority at a time (emergency fund first) protects all your other goals.

Avoid These Priority Mistakes

Spreading Money Too Thin
Saving $50 for 10 goals means no goal succeeds
Investing Before Emergency Fund
One emergency wipes out investment gains
Ignoring High-Interest Debt
20% interest destroys faster than 7% investing builds
No Starter Emergency Fund
Even $500-$1,000 prevents most financial disasters
