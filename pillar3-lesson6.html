Navigating Student Loan Debt

The Problem

The Solution

Understand federal vs private loans, repayment plans, and strategic payoff methods

Your Goal

Create a smart repayment strategy that minimizes interest and maximizes benefits

Federal vs Private Student Loans

Federal Loans (Always better when possible):

  • Fixed interest rates (usually lower)
  • Income-driven repayment plans available
  • Loan forgiveness programs (Public Service, Teacher, etc.)
  • Deferment and forbearance options in hardship
  • No credit check required for most loans

Private Loans (Last resort):

  • Variable OR fixed rates (often higher)
  • Credit check required - may need cosigner
  • No federal protections or forgiveness
  • Limited hardship options
  • May have prepayment penalties

Rule of Thumb

Always max out federal loan options before considering private loans. The protections are worth it.

Federal Repayment Plans

1. Standard Repayment (10 years): Fixed payments, pay off fastest, lowest total interest

2. Graduated Repayment (10 years): Payments start low, increase every 2 years. Good if expecting income growth

3. Extended Repayment (25 years): Lower monthly payments but much more interest over time

4. Income-Driven Repayment (20-25 years):

  • IBR (Income-Based): 10-15% of discretionary income
  • PAYE (Pay As You Earn): 10% of discretionary income
  • REPAYE: 10% of discretionary income, no income cap
  • ICR (Income-Contingent): 20% of discretionary income

Forgiveness: Income-driven plans offer loan forgiveness after 20-25 years (though forgiven amount may be taxed)

Smart Payoff Strategies

Strategy 1: Refinancing (for private loans or high-rate federal):

If you have good credit and stable income, refinancing can lower your interest rate. BUT you lose federal protections if you refinance federal loans.

Strategy 2: Avalanche Method:

Make minimum payments on all loans, put extra toward highest interest rate loan. Saves most money.

Strategy 3: Public Service Loan Forgiveness (PSLF):

Work for government or non-profit? After 120 qualifying payments on income-driven plan, remaining balance forgiven tax-free.

Strategy 4: Employer Assistance:

Many employers now offer student loan repayment as a benefit (up to $5,250/year tax-free). Always check if your employer offers this!

Your Student Loan Action Plan

Take these steps to manage student loans effectively:

Identify all your loans (federal vs private, interest rates, balances)
Choose the right repayment plan for your situation
Check if you qualify for PSLF or other forgiveness programs
Ask your employer about student loan repayment assistance
Set up autopay for 0.25% interest rate reduction on federal loans
