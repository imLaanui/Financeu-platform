Core Concept: Lifestyle Inflation

Lifestyle inflation is when your spending increases as your income grows, leaving you with no extra savings despite earning more.

How It Happens

The Trap: You get a raise from $50K to $60K. Instead of saving the extra $10K, you upgrade your apartment, lease a nicer car, eat out more‚Äîand still feel broke.

The Reality: Your new "needs" consumed the raise. You're making 20% more but saving the same (or less).

Breaking the Cycle

  • When you get a raise, immediately increase savings by 50% of the raise
  • Keep "wants" stable for 6 months before upgrading lifestyle
  • Ask: "Do I need this, or am I just celebrating income?"
  • Automate the savings increase before you see the money

The Real Cost of Saving Traps

Example: Sarah vs. Mike (Same Income Growth)

Both start at $40K/year, both get raises to $60K over 5 years.

Sarah: Avoids Lifestyle Inflation

  • Saves 50% of each raise ($500/month extra)
  • Keeps apartment and car the same
  • After 5 years: $30,000 in savings + compound interest
  • Has options: down payment, career change, security

Mike: Falls Into the Trap

  • Upgrades apartment (+$400/month), leases luxury car (+$350/month)
  • Eats out more, buys designer clothes
  • After 5 years: $2,000 in savings, stressed about money
  • Still lives paycheck-to-paycheck despite higher income

Same income. Completely different outcomes. The difference? Sarah avoided the traps.
