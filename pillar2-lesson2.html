Core Concept

The 50/30/20 rule splits your after-tax income into three categories:

  • 50% Needs: Rent, food, utilities, insurance, minimum debt payments - things you can't live without
  • 30% Wants: Dining out, hobbies, entertainment, subscriptions - things that enhance your life
  • 20% Savings & Debt: Emergency fund, retirement, extra debt payments - your future security

Example: If you earn $3,000/month after taxes:

  • $1,500 for needs
  • $900 for wants
  • $600 for savings and debt payoff

Simple Example

Meet James, who earns $4,000/month after taxes.

He allocates:

  • $2,000 for Needs (50%): Rent, groceries, utilities, car payment, insurance
  • $1,200 for Wants (30%): Gym membership, streaming services, dining out, hobbies
  • $800 for Savings (20%): Emergency fund, 401k contribution

Simple and balanced. James knows exactly how much he can spend guilt-free on wants while building his future.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Miscategorizing wants as needs: That premium cable package is a want, not a need.
  • Not adjusting for your situation: If you live in a high-cost area, your needs might be higher than 50% - that's okay, adjust accordingly.
  • Ignoring the rule entirely: Even if you can't hit the exact percentages, the principle of balancing needs, wants, and savings still applies.
  • Being too rigid: The 50/30/20 rule is a guideline, not a law. It should guide you, not stress you out.
