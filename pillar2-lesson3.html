Core Concept

An emergency fund is money set aside for unexpected expenses.

It covers things like:

  • Job loss or reduced income
  • Medical bills
  • Car repairs
  • Home repairs
  • Unexpected travel

Start with $1,000, then build to 3-6 months of expenses.

Keep it accessible (savings account) but separate from daily spending. A high-yield savings account is ideal - it earns interest but you can access it quickly.

Simple Example

Meet Maria, who decides to build an emergency fund.

She saves $100/month. In 10 months, she has $1,000.

When her car breaks down and needs an $800 repair, she doesn't panic. She uses her emergency fund instead of a credit card.

She then starts rebuilding her fund, saving another $100/month. After 8 more months, she's back to $1,000 and continues saving toward her goal of 3 months of expenses ($6,000).

The peace of mind is priceless.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using it for non-emergencies: A sale on a TV is not an emergency. Your fund is for true unexpected needs.
  • Investing it in stocks: The market could drop right when you need the money. Keep it safe and accessible.
  • Not replenishing after use: When you use your emergency fund, make it a priority to rebuild it.
  • Keeping it too accessible: Don't link it to your debit card. Make it a separate account you have to transfer from intentionally.
