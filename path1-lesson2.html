Checking accounts are designed for unlimited transactions. Swipe your debit card, write checks, pay bills online, use Venmo‚Äîdo it all, as much as you want.

Using Your Checking Account

Every bank's app and website look a bit different, but the basics are the same. Here's what you need to know:

  • Check your balance: Open your app or log in to see how much you have right now
  • View transactions: See every purchase, deposit, and withdrawal
  • Transfer money: Move between checking and savings instantly
  • Pay bills: Set up one-time or automatic payments
  • Deposit checks: Take a photo with your phone‚Äîno ATM needed

Most important habit: Check your balance before any big purchase. What the app shows is what you actually have available right now.

Smart Checking Habits

These simple habits will help you avoid fees, stay organized, and never run out of money unexpectedly:

  • Check daily: Make it a morning routine‚Äîopen the app, glance at your balance
  • Keep a buffer: Try to always keep $100-$200 extra as a cushion
  • Link to savings: Set up automatic transfers after payday
  • Review statements: Once a month, look for weird charges or subscriptions you forgot about
  • Use alerts: Low balance, large purchases, deposits‚Äîget notified for everything

Real talk: The number one reason people overdraft is not tracking pending transactions. Your app shows "available" balance, but if you have pending charges, that money is already spoken for.
