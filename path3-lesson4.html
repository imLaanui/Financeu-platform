Debt Avalanche vs Snowball

Screen 1 of 8

Two Proven Methods to Crush Debt

The Problem

Multiple debts can feel overwhelming. Which one should you pay first?

The Solution

Use either the Avalanche (save money) or Snowball (quick wins) method

Your Goal

Choose the right strategy and systematically eliminate all your debt

Screen 2 of 8

The Two Methods Explained

Debt Avalanche
Pay minimums on all debts, then put extra money toward the debt with the highest interest rate.

Best For:

  • Saving the most money
  • Math-focused people
  • Large interest rate gaps
Debt Snowball
Pay minimums on all debts, then put extra money toward the debt with the smallest balance.

Best For:

  • Quick psychological wins
  • Motivation-focused people
  • Similar interest rates

The truth: Both methods work. The best one is whichever you'll actually stick with.

Real Example: Sarah's Debt

Sarah has $500/month extra to pay down debt:

Credit Card A: $5,000 @ 24% APR
Credit Card B: $2,000 @ 18% APR
Car Loan: $8,000 @ 6% APR
Personal Loan: $3,000 @ 12% APR

Avalanche Order (highest rate first):
Card A (24%) → Card B (18%) → Personal (12%) → Car (6%)
Total interest paid: $2,847

Snowball Order (smallest balance first):
Card B ($2k) → Personal ($3k) → Card A ($5k) → Car ($8k)
Total interest paid: $3,156

The Verdict

Avalanche saves Sarah $309 in interest, but Snowball gives her 2 quick wins in the first few months. Both get her debt-free in about 2 years.

How to Choose Your Method

Choose Avalanche if:

  • You're motivated by numbers and logic
  • You have high-interest debt (20%+ credit cards)
  • Saving money is your top priority
  • You can stay motivated without quick wins

Choose Snowball if:

  • You need psychological wins to stay motivated
  • Your interest rates are all fairly similar
  • You've struggled to stick with debt plans before
  • Seeing accounts close gives you energy

The Real Secret

The method matters less than your commitment. Pick one, stick with it, and you WILL become debt-free. Switching between methods wastes time and mental energy.

Your Debt Payoff Action Plan

Follow these steps to crush your debt:

List all your debts with balances, interest rates, and minimum payments
Choose your method: Avalanche (highest rate) or Snowball (smallest balance)
Calculate how much extra you can pay beyond minimums each month
Set up automatic payments for all minimum payments (never miss one!)
Attack your target debt with all extra money while paying minimums on others
