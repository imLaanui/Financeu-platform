Building Your Emergency Fund

The Problem

Most people live paycheck to paycheck without a financial safety net

Why It Matters

An emergency fund protects you from unexpected expenses and financial stress

What You'll Learn

Why you need 3-6 months of expenses saved and how to build it step-by-step

Screen 2 of 8

Core Concept: What Is an Emergency Fund?

An emergency fund is money set aside specifically for unexpected expenses or financial emergencies.

Why 3-6 Months of Expenses?

This amount covers you during job loss, medical emergencies, major car repairs, or other unexpected costs. It prevents you from going into debt when life happens.

Where to Keep It

Keep your emergency fund in a high-yield savings account—accessible, but separate from your checking account to avoid temptation to spend it.

Start Small, Build Gradually

Don't wait until you can save 6 months. Start with $500, then $1,000, then work your way up. Even a small cushion reduces financial stress.

Real-World Example: Building Your Fund

Step 1: Calculate Monthly Expenses

  • Rent: $900
  • Groceries: $300
  • Utilities: $150
  • Transportation: $200
  • Insurance: $100
  • Total: $1,650/month

Step 2: Calculate Your Target

3 months: $1,650 × 3 = $4,950

6 months: $1,650 × 6 = $9,900

Start with the 3-month goal, then work toward 6 months.

Step 3: Build It Gradually

  • Saving $250/month = 3-month fund in ~20 months
  • Saving $500/month = 3-month fund in ~10 months
  • Every little bit helps—even $50/month makes progress!

Avoid These Mistakes

Setting Unrealistic Goals
Don't aim for 6 months right away—start with $500
Using It for Non-Emergencies
A new phone isn't an emergency—medical bills are
Keeping It Too Accessible
In your checking account? You'll spend it
Waiting to Start
"I'll save when I earn more" means never starting
Screen 7 of 8
Your Action Plan
Calculate your total monthly essential expenses
Set an initial target (start with $500 or $1,000)
Open a separate high-yield savings account for emergencies
Set up automatic monthly transfers to your emergency fund
Quick Reflection

What's one expense you could reduce this month to start building your emergency fund? Even $25 is a great start!
