Spot the Red Flags

Click each paystub scenario to see if it's safe or sketchy

Scenario A

Gross Pay: $1,500
Federal Tax: $180
FICA: $114.75
Net Pay: $1,205.25
LOOKS GOOD

All deductions are clearly itemized and reasonable. FICA is correct (7.65% of gross). Nothing sketchy here!

Scenario B

Gross Pay: $2,000
Deductions: $850
Net Pay: $1,150
No itemized breakdown
RED FLAG

Problem: Deductions must be itemized. If they just say "Deductions: $850" without showing what for, that's illegal. Ask HR for a proper breakdown!

Scenario C

Gross Pay: $1,800
Federal Tax: $216
FICA: $137.70
Uniform Fee: $200
Net Pay: $1,246.30
SUSPICIOUS

Problem: $200 uniform fee is excessive and might be illegal. Employers usually can't charge you for required uniforms if it drops you below minimum wage. Check your state laws!

Scenario D

Hours Worked: 45
Pay Rate: $15/hr
Gross Pay: $675
All paid at regular rate
RED FLAG

Problem: No overtime pay! You worked 45 hours but only got paid for 45 × $15 = $675. You should get: (40 × $15) + (5 × $22.50) = $712.50. That's wage theft!

Scenario E

Gross Pay: $2,500
Federal Tax: $300
State Tax: $125
Social Security: $155
Medicare: $36.25
401k (6%): $150
Net Pay: $1,733.75
LOOKS GOOD

Everything checks out! All deductions are itemized, FICA is correct (7.65%), 401k percentage matches, and taxes seem reasonable. This is a properly formatted paystub.

Scenario F

Gross Pay: $1,200
Cash Advance Repay: $600
Federal Tax: $72
FICA: $91.80
Net Pay: $436.20
RED FLAG

Problem: $600 cash advance repayment (50% of gross) might be illegal. Most states limit how much employers can deduct. This could drop you below minimum wage. Check your state's wage deduction laws!

If You Spot Red Flags

Don't ignore sketchy paystubs! Talk to HR first. If they don't fix it, contact your state's Department of Labor or a wage theft hotline. You have rights!

Gig Worker

Earnings: $800
Deductions: $0
Gross: $800
Net Pay: $800

No taxes taken out! Save 25-30% yourself for tax time.

Key Takeaway

Every paystub is different based on your situation, but the basics stay the same: gross pay, itemized deductions, and net pay. If yours doesn't show all three clearly, ask questions!
